STUDENT MENTAL HEALTH SURVEY - DATA DESCRIPTION
================================================

Dataset: student_mental_health_survey.csv
Total Columns: 39
Total Rows: 500
Data Type: Survey responses from university students

COLUMN DESCRIPTIONS
===================

DEMOGRAPHICS (Columns 1-7)
--------------------------
1. Age
   - Type: Numeric (some as ranges like "18-20")
   - Range: 18-25 years
   - Description: Student's age

2. Gender
   - Type: Categorical
   - Values: Male, Female
   - Description: Student's gender identity

3. University
   - Type: Categorical
   - Values: NC State University, Duke University, UNC Chapel Hill, Wake Forest
   - Description: University the student attends

4. Department
   - Type: Categorical
   - Values: Computer Science, Engineering, Business, Psychology, Biology, Arts
   - Description: Academic department/major

5. Academic Year
   - Type: Categorical
   - Values: 1st Year, 2nd Year, 3rd Year, 4th Year
   - Description: Current year of study

6. Current CGPA
   - Type: Numeric (some as ranges like "3.0-3.5")
   - Range: 2.0-4.0
   - Description: Cumulative Grade Point Average

7. Did you receive a waiver or scholarship at your university?
   - Type: Categorical
   - Values: Yes, No
   - Description: Scholarship/financial aid status

ANXIETY QUESTIONS (Columns 8-16)
---------------------------------
Rating Scale: 0 = Never, 1 = Rarely, 2 = Sometimes, 3 = Often, 4 = Very Often

8. In a semester, how often you felt nervous, anxious or on edge due to academic pressure?
9. In a semester, how often have you been unable to stop worrying about your academic affairs?
10. In a semester, how often have you had trouble relaxing due to academic pressure?
11. In a semester, how often have you been easily annoyed or irritated because of academic pressure?
12. In a semester, how often have you worried too much about academic affairs?
13. In a semester, how often have you been so restless due to academic pressure that it is hard to sit still?
14. In a semester, how often have you felt afraid, as if something awful might happen?

15. Anxiety Value
    - Type: Numeric
    - Range: 0-28 (sum of 7 anxiety questions)
    - Description: Computed anxiety score

16. Anxiety Label
    - Type: Categorical
    - Values: Low (0-17), Medium (18-34), High (35+)
    - Description: Anxiety severity classification

STRESS QUESTIONS (Columns 17-28)
---------------------------------
Rating Scale: 0 = Never, 1 = Rarely, 2 = Sometimes, 3 = Often, 4 = Very Often

17. In a semester, how often have you felt upset due to something that happened in your academic affairs?
18. In a semester, how often you felt as if you were unable to control important things in your academic affairs?
19. In a semester, how often you felt nervous and stressed because of academic pressure?
20. In a semester, how often you felt as if you could not cope with all the mandatory academic activities?
21. In a semester, how often you felt confident about your ability to handle your academic problems? (REVERSE)
22. In a semester, how often you felt as if things in your academic life are going your way? (REVERSE)
23. In a semester, how often are you able to control irritations in your academic affairs? (REVERSE)
24. In a semester, how often you felt as if your academic performance was on top? (REVERSE)
25. In a semester, how often you got angered due to bad performance or low grades that are beyond your control?
26. In a semester, how often you felt as if academic difficulties are piling up so high that you could not overcome them?

27. Stress Value
    - Type: Numeric
    - Range: 0-40 (sum of 10 stress questions)
    - Description: Computed stress score

28. Stress Label
    - Type: Categorical
    - Values: Low (0-17), Medium (18-34), High (35+)
    - Description: Stress severity classification

DEPRESSION QUESTIONS (Columns 29-39)
-------------------------------------
Rating Scale: 0 = Never, 1 = Rarely, 2 = Sometimes, 3 = Often, 4 = Very Often

29. In a semester, how often have you had little interest or pleasure in doing things?
30. In a semester, how often have you been feeling down, depressed or hopeless?
31. In a semester, how often have you had trouble falling or staying asleep, or sleeping too much?
32. In a semester, how often have you been feeling tired or having little energy?
33. In a semester, how often have you had poor appetite or overeating?
34. In a semester, how often have you been feeling bad about yourself - or that you are a failure or have let yourself or your family down?
35. In a semester, how often have you been having trouble concentrating on things, such as reading books or watching television?
36. In a semester, how often have you moved or spoke too slowly for other people to notice?
37. In a semester, how often have you had thoughts that you would be better off dead, or of hurting yourself?

38. Depression Value
    - Type: Numeric
    - Range: 0-36 (sum of 9 depression questions)
    - Description: Computed depression score

39. Depression Label
    - Type: Categorical
    - Values: Low (0-17), Medium (18-34), High (35+)
    - Description: Depression severity classification

DATA CHARACTERISTICS
====================

Sample Size: 500 students
Data Collection: Survey questionnaire
Time Frame: One academic semester
Data Quality: Synthetic data generated for research purposes

Key Relationships:
- CGPA negatively correlated with mental health scores (lower CGPA â†’ higher stress/anxiety/depression)
- Mental health scores are interconnected (high anxiety often accompanies high stress)
- Demographic factors influence mental health patterns

Missing Values: Minimal (<5% in any column)
Outliers: Handled during preprocessing

USAGE NOTES
===========

1. Age and CGPA may contain ranges (e.g., "18-20", "3.0-3.5") - convert to averages
2. Categorical variables need encoding for ML models
3. Mental health labels are derived from computed values
4. Reverse-scored questions (21-24) indicate positive coping
5. This is synthetic data for educational/research purposes

ETHICAL CONSIDERATIONS
======================

- Data privacy: No personally identifiable information
- Informed consent: Required for real survey data
- Mental health sensitivity: Results are not clinical diagnoses
- Intervention: High-risk students should be referred to professionals
